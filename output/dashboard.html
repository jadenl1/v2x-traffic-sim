Honest caveats — read before quoting.
  1. Full AADT demand oversaturates this ~2km grid ~3×; runs use a stable scale (0.2) for realistic speeds, so realised flow undershoots raw counts (demand itself is still calibrated, GEH<5 83%). A known limitation of isolated subnetworks.
  2. Adaptive signals teleport ~35% more vehicles — a few side approaches still starve past the 300s threshold. Net delay/speed still improve; this is the next fix.
  3. Baseline signals are netconvert's guessed fixed-time plans, not DDOT's real timing.
  4. V2X measured on the AM-peak window (both arms identical demand/window — deltas are fair).
